You have done the intro. The team has seen what Microsoft Copilot can do. The risk now is adoption drift — people try it once, do not see immediate value, and quietly go back to old habits. This retainer exists to make sure that does not happen.

01 — The Challenge

The intro session worked.
Now comes the hard part.

AI adoption does not fail because the technology is not good enough. It fails because organisations treat it as a one-time event rather than an ongoing change programme. The intro session was the spark. Without structure and accountability, that spark does not become a habit.

Four patterns kill AI adoption inside organisations. All four are solvable with the right ongoing support.

Adoption drift

Teams try Copilot once or twice after the intro session. Without a clear use case that fits their actual daily work, they stop. Within two weeks the habit is gone.

Uneven uptake across teams

One team uses Copilot daily. Another has not opened it since the launch. The gap creates inconsistent output quality and internal friction about whether the investment was worth it.

No internal playbook

People do not know which prompts to use, which workflows to apply AI to, or what good output looks like. Without a playbook, every person figures it out individually — and most give up.

No way to measure progress

Organisations invest in Microsoft licences but cannot show internally what the return looks like. Without a measurement framework, AI adoption stays a cost rather than a competitive advantage.
