You have done the intro. The team has seen what Microsoft Copilot can do. The risk now is adoption drift — people try it once, do not see immediate value, and quietly go back to old habits. This retainer exists to make sure that does not happen.

01 — The Challenge

The intro session worked. Now comes the hard part.

AI adoption does not fail because the technology is not good enough. It fails because organisations treat it as a one-time event rather than an ongoing change programme. The intro session was the spark. Without structure and accountability, that spark does not become a habit.

Four patterns kill AI adoption inside organisations. All four are solvable with the right ongoing support.

Adoption drift

Teams try Copilot once or twice after the intro session. Without a clear use case that fits their actual daily work, they stop. Within two weeks the habit is gone.

Uneven uptake across teams

One team uses Copilot daily. Another has not opened it since the launch. The gap creates frustration, inconsistent output quality, and internal friction about whether the investment was worth it.

No internal playbook

People do not know which prompts to use, which workflows to apply AI to, or what good output looks like. Without a playbook, every person is figuring it out individually — and most give up.

No way to measure progress

Organisations invest in Microsoft licences but cannot show internally what the return looks like. Without a measurement framework, AI adoption stays a cost rather than becoming a competitive advantage.

02 — How We Work

Advisory hours, not development hours.

This retainer is structured differently to a standard dev retainer. The work is think-work — strategy sessions, playbook design, facilitation, implementation guidance, and internal comms. The deliverable is often a framework, a policy, or a trained team, not a codebase.

01

Strategy sessions

Monthly deep-dive with Aspen leadership. We review what is working, what is not, and set the focus for the next 30 days. Outcomes are documented and shared after every session.

02

Playbook and policy design

We build internal assets your team actually uses — prompt libraries, AI policy docs, workflow guides, and use case libraries tailored to how Aspen works, not generic templates.

03

Copilot use case development

We identify, test, and document the specific Copilot workflows that create the most value for Aspen teams — and make sure those workflows actually get adopted, not just demonstrated.

04

Internal comms and change management

Adoption is a change management problem, not a technology problem. We design the internal communications that get teams bought in and stay bought in beyond the first month.

05

Async advisory channel

A dedicated Slack or WhatsApp channel where Aspen teams can ask questions, get guidance, and share wins. Real-time support between scheduled sessions.

06

Monthly adoption report

A written summary every month covering what was adopted, what shifted, what is next, and where blockers remain. Keeps leadership informed and the programme on track.
